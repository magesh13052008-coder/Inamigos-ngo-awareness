Who We Are

A Section 8 foundation built on collective action

InAmigos Foundation was founded on 23 September 2020 by Mr. Govind Shukla, who leads the organisation with a simple belief: real change happens when communities, volunteers and corporate partners work side by side. What began as a small Bilaspur-based initiative has since grown into a foundation active across 28 states.

The foundation's work is organised around six causes — feeding the hungry, educating children, caring for animals, empowering women, restoring the environment, and building employable skills through internships. Every initiative is run with the help of a growing network of volunteers, supervisors and field coordinators.

Why It Matters

The gaps we keep showing up for

Every InAmigos project responds to a specific, persistent gap in Indian communities — not a vague good intention.

Hunger in underserved communities

Many families still go without consistent access to food and basic essentials.

→ Addressed by Project Seva

Education cut short by poverty

Children from low-income households frequently drop out before gaining basic literacy or digital skills.

→ Addressed by Project Bachpanshala

Stray and vulnerable animals

Stray animals in many towns lack consistent feeding, shelter or medical attention.

→ Addressed by Project Jeev

Limited paths to women's independence

Rural women often lack access to financial independence, training, or health awareness.

→ Addressed by Project Udaan

Shrinking green cover

Deforestation and unsustainable practices continue to erode local environmental health.

→ Addressed by Project Prakriti

Youth unemployability

Many young graduates lack the practical, hands-on experience employers look for.

→ Addressed by Project Vikas
Our Work

Six roots, one foundation

Each project is named in Hindi for the value it carries — together they form a complete cycle of care, from survival to dignity to opportunity.

Seva — "Service"

Project Seva

Regular food and clothing distribution drives for homeless and underprivileged families across project cities, run entirely through volunteer effort.

50,000+ meals & essentials shared
Bachpanshala — "School of Childhood"

Project Bachpanshala

Supplementary classes, digital literacy and life-skills sessions that help underprivileged children stay in school and keep pace with their peers.

Ongoing learning circles
Jeev — "Living Being"

Project Jeev

Daily feeding rounds, rescue support and basic care for stray and injured animals, coordinated through a dedicated volunteer network.

50+ animals fed daily
Udaan — "Flight"

Project Udaan

Partnerships with rural self-help groups to build financial independence, vocational skills, and awareness around menstrual hygiene.

Self-help group partnerships
Prakriti — "Nature"

Project Prakriti

Tree plantation drives and support for eco-friendly farming practices, aimed at restoring green cover in and around project communities.

20,000+ saplings planted
Vikas — "Development"

Project Vikas

Structured internship programmes across content writing, digital marketing, research, finance and social work, building real workplace skills.

30,000+ interns trained
